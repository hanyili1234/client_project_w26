Networking & Connections

Networking: Build Connections That Open Doors

Networking isn’t about asking for a job—it’s about building genuine relationships, learning from others, and making yourself visible in your field. Strong connections often lead to internship opportunities that aren’t publicly posted.

Why Networking Matters?

  • Many internships are filled through referrals or internal recommendations.
  • Networking helps you learn about roles, companies, and career paths from real people.
  • It builds confidence and professional communication skills over time.

How to Networking

  1. Start with People You Already Know: It could be classmates, professors, teaching assistants, UMSI alumni, family, or acquaintances working in related fields
  2. Where to Connect:Linkedin, Career Fair, Student organizations, clubs, industry events
  3. How to Reach Out: When contacting someone, keep your message simple, clear, and polite.
  4. Examples Goals for Outreach: Learn about their career path, Ask what skills are important in their role, Understand how they landed their internship or job
  5. Maintaining Your Network: Networking doesn’t end after one conversation. Send a thank you note afterwards and stay in touch occasionaly.
